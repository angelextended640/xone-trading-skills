# Druckenmiller's Investment Philosophy and Strategy

## 1. Position Construction and Risk Management

### Concentrated Investing
- **"Be the pig":** Bet aggressively and concentrate when conviction is high (Source: *The New Market Wizards*, Jack Schwager, 1992)
- **Rejection of diversification:** "Diversification is the most mistaken concept they teach in business school" (Source: Lost Tree Club lecture, Jan 2015)
- **Patience strategy:** Wait for the one or two best opportunities each year, then bet big (Source: Bloomberg interview, Jun 2023)
- **Maxim:** "Put your eggs in one basket and watch the basket carefully."

### Capital Discipline
- **Capital preservation comes first:** avoid large drawdowns at all costs
- **Math of losses:** "A 50% loss requires a 100% gain to recover"
- **Asymmetric posture:**
  - Normal times: keep losses to zero or a few percent
  - High conviction: aim for +50–60% standout gains
- **Position size scales with conviction:** when you can't see clearly, don't swing the bat

### Strict Loss-Cutting
- **Exit immediately:** if the investment thesis breaks, unwind the position at once
- **Lesson from Soros:** the importance of cutting losses, learned from "the greatest cutter of losses in history"
- **Reject hedging:** "If a position needs to be hedged, you shouldn't be holding it"
- **Add to winners:** press the trade when it's working

## 2. Forming a Market View

### Macro Perspective
- **Look 18 months ahead:** "Don't invest in the present, invest in the future" (Source: Duquesne Capital Annual Letters)
- **Liquidity matters more than earnings:** "Central banks and liquidity move markets, not corporate earnings" (Source: Lost Tree Club lecture, Jan 2015)
- **Expectations matter:** "What matters is what people are going to expect about this company from here"

### Reading Central Bank Policy
- **Detecting policy mistakes:** spot when central banks have gone too far
- **Anticipating turns:** front-run major shifts in monetary policy
- **Historical examples:**
  - Early 1980s: convicted Volcker would crush inflation, concentrated in long-dated Treasuries
  - 2003–2004: wary of the Fed leaving rates too low for too long
  - 2007–2008: foresaw the housing-bubble collapse and got defensive

### Global, Multi-Asset Perspective
- **5–6 asset baskets:** equities, bonds, currencies, commodities — across the whole field
- **Choose the most attractive battlefield**
- **"Waiting is a market too":** raise cash when there is no clear opportunity
- **Volatility management:** keep daily portfolio moves at ~0.3–0.4%

## 3. Bear-Market Strategy

### Edge in Bear Markets
- **Track record:** "About 80% of my profits came in bear markets"
- **Source of opportunity:** distortions created by central-bank policy errors

### Tactics
- **Shorting equities is hard:** acknowledged explicitly
- **Long bonds:** in recessions, go long long-duration Treasuries
- **Rotate to safety:** Treasuries, gold, stable currencies
- **Exploit distortions:** excessive pessimism produces price distortions to attack

### Bear-Market Rules
1. **Spot early:** catch the signs of crisis quickly
2. **Defensive posture:** cut equity exposure rapidly
3. **Courage to be contrarian:** bet against overreaction
4. **Survival first:** avoid the big loss, live to fight another day

## 4. Quantitative Rules

### Position Sizing
- **Maximum single position:** 25% of portfolio (Maximum Conviction only)
- **Normal conviction:** 10-15% per position
- **Pilot positions:** 3-5% per position when testing a thesis

### Volatility Management
- **Daily portfolio volatility target:** 0.3-0.4% (standard mode)
- **Reduced mode (unclear signals):** 0.1-0.2% daily vol
- **Maximum concentration:** 40% in any single asset class tilt vs benchmark

### Capital Preservation Thresholds
- **Maximum drawdown tolerance:** -5% triggers defensive mode
- **Annual target structure:** 0% in bad years, +30-60% in good years
- **"Two-strike rule":** Two consecutive losing months → cut all risk to minimum

### Entry/Exit Quantitative Criteria
- **Minimum risk/reward ratio:** 3:1 for standard entries, 5:1 for concentrated bets
- **Stop-loss framework:** Time-based (thesis timeframe) + Price-based (technical invalidation)
- **Profit target:** Let winners run; only exit on thesis change, NOT price targets

## 5. Investment Mindset

### Open Mind and Flexibility
- **Listen to the market:** don't fall in love with your forecast
- **Pivot quickly:** when wrong, change direction immediately
- **Humility:** stay humble in front of the market

### Mental Discipline
- **Self-awareness:** "Know when you are hot and when you are cold"
- **Adjust by form:**
  - When cold: bunt — keep risk small
  - When hot: swing for the fences

### Long-Term Success Formula
- **Boldness paired with caution:** sharp offense, sharp defense
- **Patience:** the ability to wait for the opportunity
- **Decisiveness:** when the moment comes, take maximum risk
