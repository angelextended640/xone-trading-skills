# Druckenmiller-Style Market Analysis Guide

## Macro Analysis Framework

### 1. Reading Central Bank Policy

#### Analysis points
- **Direction of rate policy:** where in the easing / neutral / tightening cycle
- **Liquidity changes:** money supply, QE / QT dynamics
- **Policy sustainability:** look for signs of overreach
- **Gap to market expectations:** divergence between the central bank's intent and the market's understanding

#### Warning signs of a policy mistake
- Extreme low rates held for too long
- Continued easing while ignoring inflation pressure
- Policy turns that are too abrupt
- Communication failures with the market

### 2. Forecasting 18 Months Ahead

#### Forecast components
1. **Position in the economic cycle**
   - Early / mid / late expansion, slowdown, recession — where are we?
   - When is the next turning point likely?

2. **Relationship with the policy cycle**
   - Is monetary policy leading or lagging the economy?
   - How impactful is fiscal policy?

3. **Degree of market pricing**
   - Is the consensus optimistic or pessimistic?
   - Where could the surprises come from?

### 3. Global Asset Allocation

#### Per-asset-class criteria

**Equities**
- Liquidity environment (easy vs tight)
- Direction of corporate earnings (improving vs deteriorating)
- Valuation (rich vs cheap)
- Sentiment (optimistic vs pessimistic)

**Bonds**
- Rate-direction outlook
- Yield-curve shape
- Credit-spread dynamics
- Inflation expectations

**Currencies**
- Rate-differential dynamics
- Current-account position
- Political stability
- Capital-flow direction

**Commodities**
- Supply/demand balance
- USD strength/weakness
- Inflation / deflation pressure
- Geopolitical risk

## From Data to Decision (signal → action mapping)

The Strategy Synthesizer uses the following rules to convert multi-skill signals into actionable decisions. See `references/conviction_matrix.md` for the full cross-reference tables.

### Green Light Conditions (Aggressive Posture)
- Breadth composite >= 60 **AND** Uptrend zone = Bull/Strong Bull
- Market Top score < 40 (low distribution risk)
- Macro Regime = broadening **AND** confidence = high
- FTD state = FTD_CONFIRMED (if applicable)
- **Action:** 80-100% equity, concentrated positions, standard stops

### Yellow Light Conditions (Cautious Posture)
- Breadth composite 40-59 **OR** Uptrend zone = Neutral
- Market Top score 40-60 (moderate risk)
- Macro Regime = transitional
- Mixed signal convergence (convergence score 40-60)
- **Action:** 50-70% equity, reduced sizing, tighter stops

### Red Light Conditions (Defensive Posture)
- Breadth composite < 40 **OR** Uptrend zone = Bear
- Market Top score >= 60 (elevated/high risk)
- Macro Regime = contraction
- FTD state = RALLY_FAILED
- **Action:** 0-30% equity, high cash, no new entries

### Pattern-Specific Overrides
- **Policy Pivot detected:** Overweight bonds + equity even if signals are mixed (anticipate regime shift)
- **Unsustainable Distortion detected:** Override green light → reduce to yellow light minimum
- **Extreme Contrarian detected:** Override red light → allow pilot equity entries (FTD confirmation)

## Position Construction in Practice

### Conviction Assessment

#### Conditions for high conviction (bet big)
1. **Multiple factors point the same way** ("the ducks line up")
2. **Large divergence from market consensus**
3. **Highly favourable risk / reward**
4. **A clear catalyst exists**

#### Signs of low conviction (stand aside)
- Conflicting signals mixed together
- Extremely high uncertainty
- Risk / reward unclear
- Timing uncertain

### Entry and Exit Decisions

#### Entry conditions
1. **Technical confirmation:** confirm we are in the early stage of a trend
2. **Fundamentals:** investment theme is clear
3. **Sentiment:** excessive optimism or pessimism exists
4. **Risk management:** maximum loss is within tolerance

#### Exit conditions
- **Thesis gone:** the original scenario has broken down
- **Target reached:** expected profit has been achieved
- **Better opportunity:** a more attractive opportunity has appeared
- **Risk environment change:** the market environment has shifted materially

## Crisis-Response Protocol

### Signs of a Bear-Market Entry

1. **Monetary policy turning point**
   - Clear shift from easing to tightening
   - Liquidity contracting rapidly

2. **Stress in credit markets**
   - Sharp widening of credit spreads
   - Dysfunction in interbank markets

3. **Extreme bullish sentiment**
   - Everyone is bullish
   - Risk is being underpriced
   - Excessive leverage

### Crisis Action Playbook

1. **Immediate defensive posture**
   - Cut risk assets
   - Unwind leverage
   - Secure liquidity

2. **Rotate to safety**
   - Long-term Treasuries
   - Gold
   - Safe currencies (JPY, CHF, etc.)

3. **Hunt for contrarian opportunities**
   - Distortions caused by panic selling
   - Quality assets sold cheaply during flight to safety
   - Turning points created by policy response

## Day-to-Day Monitoring

### Daily checks
- Major-country rate moves
- Equity market internals (advance/decline, etc.)
- Currency-market moves
- Volatility indicators (VIX, etc.)
- Credit market dynamics

### Weekly / monthly review
- Economic-data surprises (expected vs actual)
- Tone of central-bank officials' comments
- Fund-flow data
- Positioning data
- Sentiment indicators

### Quarterly big-picture review
- Reconfirm the validity of the investment theme
- Revise the 18-month forecast
- Reassess overall portfolio risk
- Hunt for new opportunities
