# Headline Event Patterns

This reference catalogues typical market-impact patterns for different event types.
Consult it during scenario analysis to project likely future paths from analogous past events.

## 1. Monetary Policy Events

### Federal Reserve (FOMC)

#### Rate Hike
**Typical pattern:**
- **Immediate (0–1 week):** Equity market dips temporarily, bond yields rise, USD strengthens
- **Short-term (1–3 months):** Growth stocks underperform value stocks
- **Medium-term (3–12 months):** Financials firm, real estate / utilities soften
- **Long-term (12–18 months):** Economic-slowdown concerns may surface

**Sector impacts:**
| Sector | Impact | Reason |
|---------|------|------|
| Financials | + | Net interest income rises |
| Technology | – | High-valuation names see higher discount rates |
| Real Estate | – | Mortgage rates rise → demand falls |
| Utilities | – | Bond-substitute appeal weakens |
| Consumer Discretionary | – | Borrowing costs rise → consumption slows |

**Historical examples:**
- 2022 hiking cycle: Nasdaq –33%, financial sector relatively firm
- December 2018 hike: S&P 500 –9% in December, rebound in January

#### Rate Cut
**Typical pattern:**
- **Immediate:** Equity market rallies, bond yields fall, USD weakens
- **Short-term:** Growth stocks outperform
- **Medium-term:** Real estate / utilities recover
- **Long-term:** Stimulus filters into corporate earnings improvement

**Caveats:**
- A recession-driven cut can be accompanied by initial equity weakness
- "Insurance cuts" and "recession-response cuts" produce different market reactions

#### QE (quantitative easing) / QT (quantitative tightening)
**QE start:**
- Strong tailwind for equities (liquidity injection)
- All risk assets benefit
- USD weakens, commodities rise

**QT start:**
- Liquidity contraction is a headwind for equities
- Speculative assets and small caps are most affected
- USD tends to strengthen

### European Central Bank (ECB)

**Characteristics:**
- Large impact on EUR/USD FX
- Direct impact on European bank stocks
- Peripheral country spreads (Italy, Spain, etc.) are key gauges

### Bank of Japan (BOJ)

**Characteristics:**
- YCC (Yield Curve Control) policy changes have outsized market impact
- Impact on JPY carry trades
- Japanese equities (Nikkei) trend higher on JPY weakness

**2024 YCC adjustment example:**
- 10-year yield ceiling raise → JPY surges, global rates spillover

## 2. Geopolitical Events

### War / Armed Conflict

**Typical pattern:**
- **Immediate:** Equities sell off, gold rises, oil rises, govt bonds rally (flight to safety)
- **Short-term:** Defence-related stocks rise, energy stocks firm
- **Medium-term:** Supply chains dependent on the conflict region get hit
- **Long-term:** Inflation pressure, fiscal deterioration

**Sector impacts:**
| Sector | Impact | Reason |
|---------|------|------|
| Defence | ++ | Military spending increases |
| Energy | + | Supply concerns push commodity prices up |
| Airlines | – | Fuel costs up, demand down |
| Insurance | – | Geopolitical risk reserves |
| Supply chain–related | – | Logistics disruption, sourcing risk |

**Historical examples:**
- Russia-Ukraine conflict (2022): WTI crude > $130, European gas crisis
- Middle East conflict (2023): Red Sea shipping risk, freight costs rise

### Economic Sanctions

**Impact pattern:**
- Hit to companies dependent on trade with the targeted country
- Commodity-supply concerns (if the target is a major exporter)
- Tailwind for substitute suppliers

### Tariffs / Trade Frictions

**Typical pattern:**
- **Immediate:** Targeted country's equities fall, related sectors sold
- **Short-term:** Earnings concerns for export-dependent companies
- **Medium-term:** Supply-chain restructuring begins
- **Long-term:** Onshoring / friend-shoring progresses

**2018–2019 US-China trade friction example:**
- Hit to China-dependent semiconductor and farm-equipment firms
- Production shifted to Mexico / Vietnam to avoid tariffs

## 3. Regulation / Policy Changes

### Environmental Regulation

**Stricter carbon regulation:**
- **Beneficiaries:** Renewables, EVs, clean tech
- **Hit:** Oil & gas, coal, airlines, heavy industry

**Emissions trading:**
- Higher carbon credit prices → higher costs for high-emitting companies

### Financial Regulation

**Higher capital requirements:**
- Higher Tier-1 capital ratios for banks → pressure on bank profitability
- Smaller banks affected more

**Crypto-asset regulation:**
- Tighter regulation → hit to crypto-related stocks, benefit for traditional finance
- Regulatory clarity can be received positively

### Antitrust

**Blocked large M&A:**
- Target company stock falls
- Industry consolidation delayed

**Big-Tech regulation:**
- Platform-company breakup risk
- Competitors / startups benefit

## 4. Technology Shifts

### AI Revolution

**Beneficiary sectors:**
| Sector | Example tickers | Reason |
|---------|--------|------|
| Semiconductors | NVDA, AMD, AVGO | AI chip demand |
| Cloud | MSFT, AMZN, GOOGL | AI infrastructure base |
| Software | CRM, NOW, ADBE | AI feature integration |

**Affected sectors:**
| Sector | Example | Reason |
|---------|--------|------|
| Call centres | – | AI automation |
| Translation / localisation | – | AI substitution |
| Simple-task BPO | – | RPA / AI replacement |

### EV Adoption

**Beneficiaries:**
- Lithium, cobalt, nickel related
- EV charging infrastructure
- Utilities (demand growth)

**Hit:**
- Legacy auto parts (engines, transmissions)
- Gas stations
- Oil refining

### Renewable Energy

**When policy support expands:**
- Solar / wind names rally
- Demand for utility-scale storage

**When policy support recedes:**
- Clean-energy stocks fall
- Capital rotates back to legacy energy

## 5. Commodity Shocks

### Crude Oil

**Surge scenario (supply shock):**
- **Beneficiaries:** Oil majors, shale, oilfield services
- **Hit:** Airlines, transport, chemicals, consumer goods

**Collapse scenario (demand decline):**
- **Beneficiaries:** Airlines, transport, consumer goods
- **Hit:** Energy sector broadly

**Thresholds:**
- Below $80: energy stocks soft
- Above $100: inflation concerns, consumer sentiment deteriorates

### Gold

**Drivers of upside:**
- Geopolitical risk
- Inflation concerns
- Falling real rates
- USD weakness

**Related tickers:**
- Gold miners: NEM, GOLD, AEM
- Gold ETFs: GLD, IAU

### Copper

**Role as economic indicator (Dr. Copper):**
- Rising copper → expansion signal
- Falling copper → slowdown signal

**Related sectors:**
- Mining: FCX, SCCO
- Construction & infrastructure
- EVs (copper is a key input)

## 6. Corporate / M&A

### Large Acquisition Announcement

**Typical pattern:**
- Target: +20–40% (premium priced in)
- Acquirer: –3 to –10% (dilution / premium concerns)
- Peers: +5–15% (next-takeout speculation)

### Major Bankruptcy

**Systemic risk concerns:**
- Spillover across the financial sector
- Credit spreads widen

**Single-company bankruptcy:**
- Competitors benefit (market-share gains)
- Suppliers are hit

### Spin-offs

**Typical pattern:**
- Parent: short-term uncertainty, medium-term re-rating from slimming down
- Spin-off: tends to rise post-independence on growth expectations

## Caveats When Using This Reference

1. **Past patterns are a guide:** Reactions vary by market environment.
2. **Account for compound factors:** Analyse interactions, not just a single event.
3. **Timing matters:** Reaction depends on how much is already priced in.
4. **Check the magnitude:** Impact varies with the size of the event.
5. **Refresh periodically:** Update the patterns as market structure evolves.
